# Basidiomycota

## Overview

Basidiomycota — "Club Fungi" — is one of the two largest phyla in [[Kingdom Fungi]] and contains all gilled mushrooms, bracket fungi, puffballs, coral fungi, rusts, and smuts. The phylum is defined by a unique spore-bearing cell called the **basidium**.

**Species count:** ~30,000 described; potentially 1.5 million total

**Distinguishing feature:** Sexual spores (basidiospores) are produced externally on club-shaped cells called **basidia**, typically in groups of four.

## Subphyla

- **Agaricomycotina** — the mushroom-forming species, including all gilled mushrooms, bracket fungi, puffballs, and coral fungi
- **Pucciniomycotina** — primarily rust fungi (plant pathogens)
- **Ustilaginomycotina** — primarily smut fungi (plant pathogens)
- **Wallemiomycotina** — xerophilic (drought-tolerant) fungi

## Key Structural Features

- Dikaryotic hyphae with **clamp connections**
- **Dolipore septa** (cross-walls with a barrel-shaped pore surrounded by membranous caps called parenthesomes)
- Basidiospores forcibly discharged from sterigmata via the **Buller's drop** mechanism (surface tension catapult)

## Examples

- [[Agaricus bisporus]] (button mushroom)
- [[Amanita phalloides]] (death cap)
- [[Ganoderma lucidum]] (reishi)
- [[Pleurotus ostreatus]] (oyster mushroom)
- [[Psilocybe cubensis]]
- [[Boletus edulis]] (porcini)
- [[Trametes versicolor]] (turkey tail)

## Comparison with Ascomycota

| Feature | Basidiomycota | [[Ascomycota]] |
|---|---|---|
| Spore-bearing cell | Basidium (external spores) | Ascus (internal spores) |
| Spores per reproductive cell | Typically 4 basidiospores | Typically 8 ascospores |
| Spore discharge | Forcible (Buller's drop) | Explosive (turgor) or passive |
| Dikaryotic phase | **Long-lived, dominant** | Short, transient |
| Septa type | Dolipore with parenthesomes | Simple septa with Woronin bodies |
| Clamp connections | Present in many | Absent |
| Fruiting body types | Basidiocarps: agarics, brackets, puffballs, corals | Ascocarps: apothecia, perithecia, cleistothecia |
| Key examples | All gilled mushrooms, bracket fungi, puffballs | Morels, truffles, cup fungi, yeasts, Cordyceps |
| Ecological role | Primary white-rot and brown-rot decomposers; ECM | Dominant endophytes, parasites, some ECM (truffles) |
| Species count | ~30,000 | ~64,000+ |

## Notable Genera

| Genus | Key Features | Notable Species |
|---|---|---|
| *Amanita* | Universal veil producing volva + warts on cap; partial veil forming annulus; free white gills; many deadly species | [[Amanita phalloides]] (death cap), [[Amanita muscaria]] (fly agaric), [[Amanita caesarea]] (Caesar's mushroom) |
| *Agaricus* | Free gills turning pink then chocolate-brown; annulus present; often with anise/almond scent; spore print brown | [[Agaricus bisporus]] (button), [[Agaricus campestris]] (field mushroom), [[Agaricus xanthodermus]] (toxic) |
| *Pleurotus* | Eccentric to lateral stipe; decurrent gills; white spore print; white-rot on hardwoods; nematophagous | [[Pleurotus ostreatus]] (oyster), [[Pleurotus eryngii]] (king oyster) |
| *Boletus* | Pores instead of gills; stout stipe; reticulate pattern; ECM; often with trees | [[Boletus edulis]] (porcini), [[Boletus aereus]] |
| *Cantharellus* | False gills (forked ridges); fruity apricot aroma; ectomycorrhizal; cannot be cultivated | [[Cantharellus cibarius]] (golden chanterelle) |
| *Ganoderma* | Laccate (shiny) bracket; woody; white pore surface; brown spore print | [[Ganoderma lucidum]] (reishi), [[Ganoderma tsugae]] |
| *Trametes* | Thin, leathery brackets; pore surface; white-rot; multi-zoned cap | [[Trametes versicolor]] (turkey tail) |
| *Hericium* | Tooth fungi; white cascading spines; grows on hardwood wounds | [[Hericium erinaceus]] (lion's mane) |
| *Psilocybe* | Small brown mushrooms; blue staining when bruised; psilocybin/psilocin content | [[Psilocybe cubensis]], [[Psilocybe semilanceata]], [[Psilocybe azurescens]] |
| *Grifola* | Large overlapping shelf clusters; grows at base of oaks; polypore | [[Grifola frondosa]] (maitake) |
| *Inonotus* | Parasitic conk on birch; sterile mycelial sclerotium (chaga); white-rot causing | [[Inonotus obliquus]] (chaga) |
| *Lentinula* | Brown cap with white veil remnants; white gills; ECM-free; log cultivated | [[Lentinula edodes]] (shiitake) |
| *Flammulina* | Orange-brown slimy cap; velvety dark stipe; winter fruiting; dramatic wild vs. cultivated dimorphism | [[Flammulina velutipes]], [[Flammulina filiformis]] |
| *Laetiporus* | Bright orange-yellow brackets; overlapping shelves; brown-rot | [[Laetiporus sulphureus]] (chicken of the woods) |
