# Kingdom Fungi

## Overview

Kingdom Fungi constitutes one of the great kingdoms of eukaryotic life, distinct from both the plant and animal kingdoms. Fungi are heterotrophic organisms — they cannot photosynthesize and instead acquire nutrition by secreting digestive enzymes into their environment and absorbing the breakdown products. Their cell walls are composed primarily of **chitin** (a nitrogen-containing polysaccharide also found in arthropod exoskeletons), rather than cellulose as in plants. Ergosterol, rather than cholesterol, is their primary membrane sterol — a biochemical target exploited by antifungal drugs.

As of 2024, approximately **160,000 species of fungi** are formally accepted by Species Fungorum, though the estimated true diversity of the kingdom ranges from **2 to 3.8 million species**, meaning the vast majority remain undescribed. Fungi are essential to virtually all terrestrial ecosystems: they are the primary decomposers of lignocellulosic material, form symbiotic relationships with the roots of approximately 90% of land plant species, produce antibiotics and other biologically active metabolites, and serve as food for humans and countless other organisms.

## Taxonomic Hierarchy

The standard Linnaean hierarchy as applied to fungi runs:

**Kingdom → Phylum (Division) → Subphylum → Class → Order → Family → Genus → Species**

Example using [[Agaricus bisporus]] (the common button mushroom):

- Kingdom: **Fungi**
- Phylum: **[[Basidiomycota]]**
- Subphylum: **Agaricomycotina**
- Class: **Agaricomycetes**
- Order: **Agaricales**
- Family: **Agaricaceae**
- Genus: *Agaricus*
- Species: *Agaricus bisporus*

## Major Phyla

Modern molecular phylogenetics, particularly genome-scale phylogenies published between 2013 and 2024, have reorganized the Kingdom Fungi from the classical four-phylum model into a **nine-phylum framework**. The classical "Zygomycota" has been demonstrated to be paraphyletic (not a natural group) and has been split across multiple phyla.

The nine major phyla are:

1. [[Basidiomycota]] — "Club Fungi" (~30,000 described species)
2. [[Ascomycota]] — "Sac Fungi" (~64,000–100,000+ described species)
3. Mucoromycota — formerly part of "Zygomycota" (~1,000–1,500 described)
4. Glomeromycota — arbuscular mycorrhizal fungi (~300–350 described)
5. Chytridiomycota — "Chytrids" (~1,000+ described)
6. Blastocladiomycota (~180 described)
7. Neocallimastigomycota (~20 described)
8. Zoopagomycota (several hundred)
9. Opisthosporidia (Microsporidia + allies, 1,500+ Microsporidia described)

See [[Minor Phyla]] for full details on phyla 3–9, and [[Classification Methods]] for the history and methodology of fungal classification.
