## Identification

- **Fruiting body:** Forms a single clump without cap or stipe differentiation; entire structure consists of cascading, downward-pointing white spines (icicle-like), each >1 cm long; overall fruiting body 10–40 cm across
- **Color:** White when fresh; turns yellow to orange-brown with age or on drying
- **Flesh:** White; firm; resembles seafood (crab or lobster) in texture
- **Spore print:** White
- **Spores:** Amyloid (stain blue with Melzer's reagent), smooth, 5–7 × 4–5.5 μm

No other common mushroom looks quite like *H. erinaceus* — the cascading white spines are diagnostic.

*See also: [[Morphological Identification]] (teeth/spine hymenium), [[Spore Prints]] (white spore print, amyloid spores), [[Foraging Safety]], [[Habitat Guide]] (deadwood, hardwood associations)*

## Biology

[[Wood Decay|White-rot]] saprotroph. Causes **white heart rot** in host trees. Occurs in late summer and autumn on wounds of living trees and dead wood. Both saprotrophic and opportunistically parasitic. Originally described by Bulliard; transferred to *Hericium* by Persoon in 1797.

## Cultivation

Highly cultivated commercially. Grows on [[Substrates|hardwood sawdust]] blocks (oak, beech, maple) supplemented with wheat bran or oat bran. Requires cooler [[Environmental Parameters]]: fruiting at 15–22°C, high humidity (85–95% RH), and good fresh air exchange. Low CO₂ environment is essential — high CO₂ causes the spines to elongate and clump rather than cascading freely. Colonization is relatively slow but the fruiting body is distinctive and high-value.

## Bioactive Compounds

- **[[Hericenones and Erinacines|Hericenones]]** (C–H variants): found in fruiting bodies; benzaldehyde derivatives with NGF-stimulating activity
- **[[Hericenones and Erinacines|Erinacines]]** (A–K, P–S; 15 identified): cyathin diterpenoids found in mycelium; these are the primary **NGF (Nerve Growth Factor)-stimulating** compounds. Erinacines cross the blood-brain barrier and stimulate NGF synthesis in the hippocampus and cerebellum. NGF promotes survival, maintenance, and regeneration of neurons.
- **[[Beta-Glucans]]**: immunomodulatory polysaccharides
- **[[Ergothioneine]]**: present in lion's mane (see [[Nutritional Profiles]])
- Research focus areas: Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease, peripheral nerve regeneration, depression, and anxiety

### Species-Specific Health Research

**Cognitive function:**

- **Key 2009 RCT (Mori et al., Japan):** Double-blind, placebo-controlled, 16-week trial. 30 patients with mild cognitive impairment (ages 50–80). Intervention: 250 mg tablets of 96% dried powder, 3 tablets × 3 times daily = **3g/day**. Results: Significant improvement on Hasegawa's Dementia Scale at weeks 8, 12, and 16 vs. placebo (p<0.01 at each point). Importantly, scores declined after the 4-week washout period, suggesting the effect was dependent on continued use.
- **2023 RCT (Healthy young adults, N=41):** 28-day trial using 1.8g/day lion's mane. Found a trend toward reduced subjective stress (p=0.051 — just missed significance). Cognitive improvements in specific tasks were observed acutely.
- **2025 Frontiers in Nutrition double-blind RCT:** Standardized extract in healthy young adults; acute effects on cognition and mood assessed; results support modest short-term cognitive benefits.
- **2023 pilot (overweight adults, N=77):** 8 weeks supplementation → 29.4% improvement in depression scores, 33.2% reduction in anxiety, 39.1% improvement in sleep disorders vs. baseline (note: baseline-only comparison limits interpretation).

**Nerve regeneration (preclinical only as of 2024):**
- Erinacine A at 300 mg/kg in stroke models: **44% infarct volume reduction**
- Erinacine A reduced amyloid-β plaques and increased insulin-degrading enzyme (IDE) expression by 130–303% in Alzheimer's disease models
- In peripheral nerve injury models, *H. erinaceus* extract accelerated nerve regeneration and functional recovery
- Reversal of MPTP-induced motor deficits in Parkinson's disease models
- Normalized BDNF pathways in depression models

**Depression and anxiety:**
- 4-week RCT in 30 menopausal women: 4 × 500mg lion's mane daily showed significant reduction in depression vs. placebo
- 2023 systematic review acknowledges "small and short-duration clinical trials" with mixed results

**Critical compound distinction:**
- **Erinacines** are found almost exclusively in **mycelium**, not fruiting body
- **Hericenones** are found primarily in **fruiting body**
- A product claiming neuroprotection should specify which part of the mushroom it uses. See [[Hericenones and Erinacines]] for full detail.

**Assessment:** Promising neuroprotective profile with strong mechanistic preclinical data. Human RCT evidence is real but limited by small sample sizes and short durations. The most credible human evidence is for mild cognitive impairment in the elderly. Larger phase 2/3 trials are needed.

## Culinary

Mild, sweet flavor reminiscent of crab or lobster — makes it an excellent seafood substitute. The firm white flesh holds up well to sautéing, roasting, and braising. Best cooked at high heat until golden brown; avoid boiling or steaming, which makes the texture spongy. Excellent as a main dish, in risotto, or as a meat substitute.
