# *Fusarium venenatum* (Quorn Fungus)

## Identification

*F. venenatum* is a filamentous microfungus — not a mushroom. It produces no macroscopic fruiting body.

- **Colony morphology**: Moderate growth rate; pale to salmon-pink colonies on agar; cottony to powdery texture
- **Microscopy**: Produces microconidia (small, oval) and macroconidia (sickle-shaped, multiseptate — the characteristic *Fusarium* morphology)
- **In production**: Grows as a dispersed mycelial suspension in submerged fermentation, not as visible colonies

## Biology

Discovered in a soil sample from a field in Buckinghamshire, UK, during a 1960s screening program for organisms that could convert starch into protein. After 20 years of R&D and extensive safety testing (including long-term human feeding studies), commercial mycoprotein production began under the Quorn brand.

**Key biological features:**
- Filamentous growth produces long, branching hyphae with fibrous texture resembling animal muscle tissue
- Grows well on simple carbon sources (glucose from hydrolyzed starch)
- **Morphological instability**: During extended continuous fermentation, highly branched colonial mutants emerge, limiting production run length to ~1,000 hours (~5 weeks)
- Cell walls contain both **beta-glucan** and **chitin**, contributing to the fiber content of the final product

## Cultivation

*F. venenatum* is produced via **continuous submerged fermentation** — the most sophisticated fungal fermentation process in the food industry:

- **Fermenters**: 150,000-liter pressure-cycle airlift fermenters, 40 meters tall
- **Output**: ~300 kg biomass per hour
- **Run length**: ~1,000 hours (~5 weeks), limited by morphological mutant accumulation
- **Carbon source**: Glucose (from hydrolyzed wheat starch or corn syrup)
- **Nitrogen source**: Ammonium salts and biotin

**Post-fermentation processing:**
1. RNA reduction via **heat treatment** (reduces RNA to below 2% — critical for safety, as high dietary RNA causes elevated uric acid/gout)
2. Steaming and chilling
3. Freezing — creates the fibrous texture characteristic of Quorn products

## Culinary

Quorn products (chicken pieces, mince, nuggets, fillets) are available in 18+ countries. The fibrous hyphal texture — an intrinsic property of filamentous fungal growth, not an engineered feature — microscopically resembles chicken breast. No extrusion or texturizing agents required (unlike pea/soy protein isolates).
