## Identification

- **Fruiting body:** Thin (2–5 mm thick), leathery, fan-shaped to semicircular brackets, typically **2–10 cm across**, growing in overlapping tiled clusters (imbricate)
- **Upper surface:** Distinctively **multi-zoned in concentric bands of varying colors** — brown, tan, buff, gray, cream, occasionally bluish or greenish (algal colonization); surface finely hairy (tomentose)
- **Pore surface:** White to cream; tiny pores (3–5 per mm)
- **Flesh:** White; very thin; tough and leathery (inedible as food due to texture)
- **Spore print:** White; spores cylindrical, 4.5–8 × 1.5–2.5 μm

**Synonym:** *Coriolus versicolor* (the name appears in older medical literature for PSK studies)

**[[Dangerous Look-Alikes]]:**
- *Stereum ostrea* (false turkey tail): similar banded appearance but **NO pores** on the underside — smooth underside; not medically active
- Various *Trametes* species: check pore surface carefully; true turkey tail always has distinct tiny pores (use a hand lens)

## Biology

[[Wood Decay|White-rot]] saprotroph; one of the most ecologically important wood decomposers. Produces abundant laccases and manganese peroxidases. Found in temperate forests worldwide on dead hardwood, playing a critical role in carbon cycling.

First described by Linnaeus as *Boletus versicolor*; moved to *Trametes* by Lloyd in 1920. *Versicolor* means "of various colors," referring to the multicolored concentric bands.

## Cultivation

Can be cultivated on [[Substrates|hardwood sawdust]] or logs. Grows readily in laboratory and commercial settings. Colonizes aggressively at room temperature. Primarily cultivated for medicinal extract production rather than food.

## Bioactive Compounds

- **PSK (Polysaccharide-K / Krestin)**: protein-bound polysaccharide; 25–38% protein, remainder [[Beta-Glucans|beta-glucan]]; extracted from a specific strain (*Coriolus versicolor* CM-101); **approved in Japan as an adjuvant cancer therapy** (available since 1977); used alongside conventional chemotherapy for gastric, colorectal, and breast cancers; stimulates NK cell activity, promotes IL-2 and IFN-γ production
- **PSP (Polysaccharopeptide)**: developed in China; ~60% polysaccharide, 10–30% peptide; similar immunomodulatory profile to PSK; used in colorectal cancer treatment
- **Other compounds**: ergosterol, coriolan, various lectins

**PSK is among the best-studied mushroom-derived pharmaceuticals in the world**, with multiple large-scale randomized controlled trials in Japan demonstrating survival benefit as an adjuvant to chemotherapy in gastric and colorectal cancer. PSP has similar evidence from Chinese clinical trials.

## Culinary

Turkey tail is too tough and leathery to eat as food. Traditionally consumed as a decoction (simmered in water for 20–60 minutes), as a tea, or as an extract. The tea has a mild, earthy flavor. Commercial preparations include capsules, powders, and liquid extracts.
