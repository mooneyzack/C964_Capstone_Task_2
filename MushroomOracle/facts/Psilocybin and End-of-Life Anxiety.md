End-of-life anxiety and depression are among the most robustly studied therapeutic indications for psilocybin. Two landmark parallel RCTs, both published in the Journal of Psychopharmacology in 2016, provided convergent evidence from separate research teams at NYU Langone Health and Johns Hopkins University.

The NYU study (Ross et al.) enrolled 29 patients with advanced cancer. A single dose of psilocybin versus niacin (active placebo) in a crossover design found that 80% of the psilocybin group achieved significant relief from distress — with effects lasting more than 6 months after a single session. The Johns Hopkins parallel study (Griffiths et al.) enrolled 51 cancer patients comparing high doses (22 or 30 mg/70kg) versus very low doses (1 or 3 mg/70kg). At 6-month follow-up, 78–83% of the high-dose group showed clinically significant decreases in depressed mood and anxiety. A 2020 long-term NYU follow-up study (Journal of Psychopharmacology) with 79 men and women from two prior trials documented significant reductions in anxiety, depression, anger, and physical distress symptoms lasting up to six months.

Mystical-type experiences appear to mediate the therapeutic effect — scores on the Mystical Experience Questionnaire correlated with therapeutic outcomes in these and subsequent studies, suggesting that the quality of the subjective experience (rather than simply the pharmacological effect) is the key driver. The mechanistic basis may involve psilocin's disruption of Default Mode Network (DMN) connectivity and its high-affinity TrkB-BDNF receptor binding, which promotes lasting neuroplastic changes supporting emotional resilience.

**Key evidence:**
- NYU 2016 RCT (Ross et al., N=29 cancer patients): 80% achieved significant distress relief lasting >6 months after single session
- Johns Hopkins 2016 RCT (Griffiths et al., N=51 cancer patients): 78–83% showed clinically significant anxiety/depression reduction at 6 months (high-dose group)
- NYU 2020 long-term follow-up (N=79): reductions in anxiety, depression, anger, physical distress lasting up to 6 months
- Mystical Experience Questionnaire scores predict therapeutic outcome
- Crossover design in NYU study allowed all participants to eventually receive psilocybin
- Both studies used therapist support and prepared set/setting protocols alongside drug administration

**Context for content creators:**
This is among the most emotionally compelling and human-centered evidence in psychedelic research. The end-of-life context is deeply relatable and largely free of the stigma that can accompany depression or addiction treatment. Key caveats: these are Phase 2 studies with relatively small samples; the therapy context (set, setting, and therapist support) is integral to outcomes — psilocybin without these protocols is not the same intervention. Do not suggest that recreational use produces therapeutic outcomes; the structured therapeutic container is a key part of what makes these trials successful. The 78–83% response rate figures are for high-dose sessions; the low-dose control groups showed far smaller effects.
