Psilocybin is a prodrug that is rapidly converted to psilocin (4-OH-DMT) in the body. As the active compound, psilocin is a primary agonist at 5-HT2A receptors in cortical pyramidal neurons, triggering glutamate release, mTOR pathway activation, and BDNF expression. A 2023 Nature Neuroscience finding revealed a second, independent mechanism: psilocin binds the TrkB receptor (the primary BDNF receptor) with an affinity approximately 1,000× higher than conventional antidepressants, promoting synaptogenesis and dendritic arborization independent of 5-HT2A activation.

The FDA granted Breakthrough Therapy Designation for psilocybin in treatment-resistant depression (TRD) in August 2018 (to COMPASS Pathways) and for major depressive disorder in November 2019 (to Usona Institute). In the COMPASS Pathways Phase 2b RCT (2022, N=233 patients with TRD across 10 countries), the 25mg dose produced significant MADRS depression score reductions at 3 weeks (primary endpoint), with 29.1% remission versus 7.6% in the 1mg control group. A parallel 2021 Imperial College London RCT (Carhart-Harris et al., NEJM, N=59) compared psilocybin (two 25mg doses) against escitalopram (20mg/day for 6 weeks); both groups improved comparably on the primary outcome, with psilocybin showing trends for better performance on emotional blunting and wellbeing scales.

Neuroplasticity data suggests why effects outlast the acute pharmacological window: a single psilocybin dose produces dendritic spine density increases lasting at least one month in preclinical models — substantially longer than ketamine's 24-hour effect. Default Mode Network (DMN) disruption during the session and subsequent reorganization toward healthier connectivity patterns are proposed as the mechanism underlying lasting therapeutic effects. As of 2026, 134+ clinical trials are registered on ClinicalTrials.gov spanning 54 potential indications; no marketing approvals have been granted.

**Key evidence:**
- FDA Breakthrough Therapy: TRD (August 2018, COMPASS Pathways); MDD (November 2019, Usona Institute)
- COMPASS Phase 2b RCT (2022, N=233): 29.1% remission at 3 weeks (25mg) vs. 7.6% (1mg control)
- Imperial College/NEJM 2021 (N=59): psilocybin vs. escitalopram — comparable primary outcome, trends favoring psilocybin on secondary measures
- Psilocin binds TrkB (BDNF receptor) at Ki ~6.7 nM — ~1,000× higher affinity than conventional antidepressants (2023 Nature Neuroscience)
- Dendritic spine density increases lasting ≥1 month post-single dose (preclinical)
- 134+ registered trials, 0 marketing approvals as of 2026

**Context for content creators:**
The FDA Breakthrough Therapy Designation is a legitimate factual anchor — but clarify this means the FDA recognizes early evidence is promising and wants to expedite review, not that psilocybin is approved for clinical use. Psilocybin remains Schedule I in the US (with exceptions for Oregon and Colorado supervised services programs). Phase 3 trial results are still pending. The COMPASS Phase 2b data is the strongest human RCT evidence; frame the 29.1% remission figure accurately: this is at 3 weeks, in a Phase 2 (not Phase 3) trial. Avoid the words "cure" or "approved treatment" in any US-context content.
