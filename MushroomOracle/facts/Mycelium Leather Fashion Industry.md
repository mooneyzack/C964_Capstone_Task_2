Mycelium leather is grown by cultivating *Ganoderma* species mycelium on agricultural waste substrates (corn stalks, hemp hurds) in controlled temperature and humidity conditions for 1–2 weeks. The colonized mat is compressed, pressed to the desired thickness and density, heat-killed to halt growth, and surface-treated with plant-based tannins and finishing agents. The result is a flexible, workable material with a texture and drape that resembles animal leather.

Two companies lead the field. Bolt Threads developed "Mylo" and partnered with Stella McCartney (a one-of-a-kind Mylo bag exhibited at the Victoria and Albert Museum, London, 2021), Adidas (prototype sneakers), Lululemon, and the Kering luxury group (Gucci, Balenciaga parent). MycoWorks developed "Fine Mycelium" — a denser, more isotropic material grown under controlled compression — and partnered with Hermès in 2021 to produce the "Sylvania" Victoria bag, a limited-edition piece that represents the most prestigious luxury fashion adoption of a myco-material to date.

The Hermès collaboration required MycoWorks to scale to commercial production volumes while maintaining the exacting quality standards demanded by one of the world's most technically demanding luxury brands. Both Mylo and Reishi materials are grown at ambient temperature from renewable agricultural feedstocks, are animal-free (vegan), and are far less carbon-intensive than bovine leather production.

**Key evidence:**
- MycoWorks + Hermès "Sylvania" Victoria bag (2021): highest-profile luxury validation of mycelium leather
- Bolt Threads + Stella McCartney Mylo bag at V&A Museum (2021): significant fashion world credibility
- Bolt Threads + Adidas + Lululemon + Kering: breadth of fashion industry interest
- Current limitations: durability still below full-grain bovine leather in abrasion resistance; cost 2–5× conventional leather; scaling to hundreds of thousands of square meters/year is a manufacturing challenge
- No PU coating versions are in development to eliminate the last synthetic component

**Context for content creators:**
The Hermès partnership is the strongest credibility signal for this technology — luxury brands are notoriously conservative about materials innovation. Caveat: production at scale remains limited; these are primarily proof-of-concept and limited-edition products. The technology is real but not yet in mass-market production. Do not imply mycelium leather has replaced conventional leather at scale.
