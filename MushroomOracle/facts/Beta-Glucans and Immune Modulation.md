Beta-glucans are polysaccharides composed of D-glucose monomers linked by β-type glycosidic bonds. Fungal beta-glucans are structurally distinct from the beta-glucans in oats and barley: mushroom beta-glucans feature a β-1,3 backbone with β-1,6 branching side chains that create a triple-helix tertiary conformation. This triple helix is recognized by innate immune pattern-recognition receptors as a pathogen-associated molecular pattern (PAMP), triggering a coordinated immune response.

The primary receptor is Dectin-1 (a C-type lectin-like receptor), expressed on monocytes, macrophages, neutrophils, and dendritic cells. When beta-glucan binds Dectin-1, it activates spleen tyrosine kinase (Syk) and the CARD9-Bcl10-MALT1 complex, triggering NF-κB activation, cytokine production (TNF-α, IL-6, IL-12, IL-23), and enhanced phagocytosis. A second mechanism — CR3-dependent cytotoxicity — occurs after macrophages digest beta-glucan particles into smaller fragments that enter the lymphatic system and bind CR3 on neutrophils and NK cells, priming them for enhanced cytotoxicity against antibody-opsonized tumor cells. TLR-2 and TLR-6 co-activation potentiates cytokine output.

In human clinical evidence, a Cochrane-registered RCT (Dai et al., 2015) found that healthy adults consuming shiitake mushrooms daily for 4 weeks showed significantly improved immunity markers: increased gamma-delta T-cells and NK cells, elevated secretory IgA, decreased CRP, and improved cytokine profiles. A 2016 Cochrane review of reishi in cancer patients (N=373) documented CD3 T-cells +3.91%, CD4 T-cells +3.05%, and CD8 T-cells +2.02% increases.

**Key evidence:**
- Dectin-1 (C-type lectin-like receptor): primary beta-glucan receptor on innate immune cells
- NF-κB activation: TNF-α, IL-6, IL-12, IL-23 cytokine induction; enhanced phagocytosis
- CR3-dependent cytotoxicity: primes NK cells and neutrophils against antibody-opsonized tumor cells
- Shiitake RCT (Dai et al., 2015): increased NK cells, gamma-delta T-cells, secretory IgA; decreased CRP
- Reishi Cochrane review (2016, N=373): CD3 +3.91%, CD4 +3.05%, CD8 +2.02% in cancer patients
- Beta-glucan content varies by species: shiitake stalk 25.3% DW, oyster 24.2% DW, white button cap 8.6% DW

**Context for content creators:**
"Immune modulation" is the accurate term — not "immune boost." Beta-glucans prime and regulate the immune system; they do not simply ramp it up. This matters for people with autoimmune conditions (where immune boosting could worsen symptoms) and for those on immunosuppressant drugs (potential interaction). Beta-glucans require hot water extraction to become bioavailable — raw mushrooms and mycelium-on-grain supplements with no extraction may deliver far fewer active compounds. When evaluating supplements, look for products listing beta-glucan content specifically (not just "polysaccharides").
