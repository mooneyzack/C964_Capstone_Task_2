Lentinan is a highly purified β-1,3-D-glucan with β-1,6 branches derived from Lentinula edodes (shiitake mushroom). Unlike the crude beta-glucan fractions in whole mushroom preparations, lentinan is a pharmaceutical-grade extract approved as an adjuvant cancer therapy in Japan since 1985 (produced by Ajinomoto Corp). It is administered intravenously at 1–1.5 mg/day for 2–8 weeks, typically in combination with chemotherapy rather than as a standalone treatment.

Clinical evidence for lentinan is among the strongest in medicinal mycology. A meta-analysis of trials in advanced gastric cancer (N=650 patients) demonstrated that IV lentinan combined with chemotherapy significantly prolonged survival compared to chemotherapy alone. Lung cancer trials showed improved response rates of 56% vs. 43.3% in the lentinan adjunct group. The mechanism involves CR3-dependent cytotoxicity — after macrophage phagocytosis of lentinan particles, smaller glucan fragments enter the lymphatic circulation and bind Complement Receptor 3 (CR3) on neutrophils and NK cells, priming them for enhanced cytotoxicity against antibody-opsonized tumor cells.

Beyond its cancer adjuvant role, lentinan contributes to the broader immunomodulatory profile of shiitake. A Cochrane-registered RCT (Dai et al., 2015) in healthy young adults consuming whole shiitake mushrooms daily for 4 weeks found significantly improved immunity markers: proliferation of gamma-delta T-cells and NK cells, increased secretory IgA, decreased CRP (anti-inflammatory), and improved cytokine profiles. Shiitake also ranks first among 26 edible and medicinal mushrooms tested for HMG-CoA reductase inhibitory activity.

**Key evidence:**
- Lentinan approved as injectable cancer adjuvant in Japan since 1985 (Ajinomoto Corp; IV 1–1.5 mg/day)
- Meta-analysis, N=650 advanced gastric cancer patients: lentinan + chemo significantly prolonged survival vs. chemo alone
- Lung cancer: 56% vs. 43.3% response rates with lentinan adjunct
- Mechanism: CR3-dependent cytotoxicity via macrophage phagocytosis → NK cell and neutrophil priming
- Whole shiitake RCT (Dai et al., 2015): increased NK cells, gamma-delta T-cells, IgA; decreased CRP in healthy adults
- Shiitake ranks #1 of 26 mushroom species for HMG-CoA reductase inhibitory activity

**Context for content creators:**
Lentinan is a pharmaceutical drug approved in Japan — not a supplement. This distinction is important: the clinical evidence applies to IV-administered pharmaceutical lentinan alongside chemotherapy, not to eating shiitake mushrooms or taking oral shiitake supplements. The Dai et al. whole-mushroom RCT in healthy adults is more directly relevant to food/supplement content and is a legitimate anchor for immune health claims. Always clarify "approved in Japan" (not FDA-approved as a drug). The HMG-CoA reductase inhibitory activity (#1 of 26 species) is an interesting hook for cardiovascular content, but this is in vitro data and should not be presented as equivalent to clinical statin evidence.
