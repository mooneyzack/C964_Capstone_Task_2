Ergothioneine (ERG) is a sulfur-containing, histidine-derived amino acid synthesized exclusively by fungi and certain actinobacteria. Plants and animals — including humans — lack the enzymatic machinery to produce it and must obtain it through diet. Mushrooms accumulate ERG at levels orders of magnitude higher than any other food: golden oyster mushroom (*Pleurotus citrinopileatus*) reaches 10.65 mg/g dry weight, porcini (*Boletus edulis*) exceeds 7.0 mg/g, and common oyster mushroom reaches up to 4.0 mg/g — compared to essentially trace levels in most plant foods.

Humans evolved a highly specific, high-affinity transporter for ergothioneine — the SLC22A4 protein (previously OCTN1) — expressed across liver, kidney, intestine, blood cells, and brain. ERG concentrates in erythrocytes, liver, and the lens of the eye. The existence of a dedicated transporter that evolved under selective pressure is the primary scientific argument that ERG may have vitamin-like status — the body would not maintain an energetically costly high-affinity uptake mechanism for a compound it doesn't need. This argument parallels the reasoning behind vitamin status designations for other essential compounds.

Epidemiological associations are compelling: a Swedish cohort study (N=3,236) found higher plasma ERG associated with 15% lower coronary disease risk (HR=0.85; p=0.01), 21% lower cardiovascular mortality (HR=0.79; p=0.002), and 14% lower all-cause mortality (HR=0.86; p=4×10⁻⁵). A Singapore study found consuming 1.5 mushroom servings per week was associated with halved incidence of mild cognitive impairment; 9 servings/week was associated with a five-fold decrease. ERG is heat-stable and survives cooking, making cooked mushrooms a reliable dietary source.

**Key evidence:**
- Only fungi and certain actinobacteria synthesize ergothioneine; no plant or mammal can produce it
- Content by species: golden oyster 10.65 mg/g DW; porcini >7.0 mg/g; oyster up to 4.0 mg/g; shiitake ~1.0 mg/g; white button ~0.4 mg/g
- Dedicated human transporter SLC22A4 evolved under selective pressure — suggests essential function
- Swedish cohort (N=3,236): higher plasma ERG → 15% lower coronary risk, 21% lower cardiovascular mortality, 14% lower all-cause mortality
- Singapore study: 1.5 mushroom servings/week → halved mild cognitive impairment incidence
- ERG is GRAS-designated; heat-stable; survives cooking

**Context for content creators:**
The "longevity vitamin" framing comes from researcher Bruce Ames and is compelling but not yet consensus — ERG has not been officially classified as a vitamin. Epidemiological associations between plasma ERG and reduced mortality are strong, but these are observational studies, not RCTs; people who eat more mushrooms may differ from non-consumers in other health behaviors. The transporter argument is the strongest mechanistic hook. A fair claim: "Mushrooms are by far the richest dietary source of ergothioneine, a compound some researchers propose as a longevity vitamin." Avoid stating that mushroom ERG has been proven to extend lifespan.
