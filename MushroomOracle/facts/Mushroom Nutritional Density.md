Mushrooms are approximately 80–90% water by fresh weight. While a 100g serving of raw button mushrooms delivers only 3.1g protein and 22 kcal, the dry-weight picture is substantially more impressive: protein content across edible species ranges from 13.8 to 38.5g per 100g dry weight, carbohydrates from 32 to 61.4g/100g DW, and fat from 0.4 to 5.9g/100g DW. Oyster mushrooms run approximately 33 kcal and 3.3g protein per 100g fresh weight; on a dry-weight basis, oyster mushrooms can reach the higher end of the protein range.

Critically, mushroom protein contains all nine essential amino acids (EAAs), making it technically a complete protein source — a property most plant proteins lack. The Protein Digestibility-Corrected Amino Acid Score (PDCAAS) for cooked mushrooms ranges from 0.7 to 1.2 depending on species and preparation, with 1.0 being the maximum. Studies on Agaricus bisporus and Lentinula edodes confirm all nine EAAs are present in cooked forms. However, digestibility from raw mushrooms is limited by chitin — the tough β-1,4-linked N-acetylglucosamine polymer in fungal cell walls that human enzymes cannot break down. Cooking (heat and acid) breaks down chitin, substantially improving protein bioavailability: cooked mushrooms achieve 84% degree of hydrolysis versus ~38% for raw.

Beyond protein, mushrooms deliver meaningful B vitamins (riboflavin 31% RDA, niacin 23% RDA, pantothenic acid 30% RDA per 100g), selenium (17% RDA), copper (34% RDA), and potassium (7% RDA) per standard serving — plus ergothioneine (absent in plants) and beta-glucans, making them genuinely nutrient-dense across multiple dimensions.

**Key evidence:**
- Dry-weight protein: 13.8–38.5g/100g DW across edible species
- All nine essential amino acids present in cooked mushrooms (confirmed in A. bisporus and L. edodes)
- PDCAAS for cooked mushrooms: 0.7–1.2 (comparable to many animal proteins)
- Raw mushroom digestibility: ~38% protein hydrolysis; cooked: ~84% (chitin breakdown via heat)
- Standard 100g button mushroom: riboflavin 31% RDA, niacin 23% RDA, copper 34% RDA
- Mushrooms are also the primary dietary source of ergothioneine — absent from plant foods

**Context for content creators:**
The "complete protein" and high dry-weight protein claims are accurate and powerful for plant-based and vegetarian audiences. Critical nuances: always cook mushrooms — raw mushrooms have poor protein bioavailability due to chitin. The 38.5% protein dry-weight figure applies to certain species under optimal conditions; common button mushrooms are on the lower end (~13–15% DW). The PDCAAS range of 0.7–1.2 is comparable to many animal proteins, which is meaningful context for plant-based protein conversations. Avoid citing fresh-weight protein content (3g/100g) without the dry-weight context — it undersells the genuine nutritional density.
