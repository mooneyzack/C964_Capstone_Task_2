# Precision Fermentation

Precision fermentation uses genetically engineered microorganisms (GEMs) — often filamentous fungi or yeasts — to produce specific target molecules with high purity. Unlike traditional fermentation (see [[Koji Fermentation]], [[Tempeh and Rhizopus]]), which relies on natural organisms producing complex mixtures of metabolites, precision fermentation inserts genes encoding a desired protein into a host organism, which then produces large amounts of that single compound during fermentation. The target molecule is then isolated and purified.

---

## Traditional vs. Precision Fermentation

| Dimension | Traditional Fermentation | Precision Fermentation |
|-----------|------------------------|----------------------|
| Organisms | Natural or minimally modified | Genetically engineered |
| Output | Complex mixture (biomass, metabolites, substrate) | Single target molecule, high purity |
| Cost | Low, simple processing | Higher, requires purification |
| Scalability | Established (millennia of practice) | Rapidly scaling |
| Environmental impact | Good | Estimated 70% less GHG, 95% less land, 80% less water vs. animal agriculture |

## Key Companies Using Fungal Hosts

### Perfect Day — Whey Protein from *Trichoderma reesei*

Produces **beta-lactoglobulin** (whey protein) via submerged fermentation of engineered *T. reesei* (see [[Biotech Applications]] for *T. reesei*'s history as an industrial enzyme producer). The fungus secretes the protein into the fermentation broth. Post-fermentation:

1. Culture centrifuged to separate biomass
2. Protein concentrated via ultrafiltration/diafiltration
3. Spray-dried to powder

The resulting protein is **molecularly identical to cow's milk beta-lactoglobulin**. FDA GRAS "no questions" letter received (GRN No. 863). Products include ice cream, cream cheese, and protein powder under various brand partnerships.

### The EVERY Company (formerly Clara Foods)

Uses precision fermentation to produce **ovalbumin** (egg white protein) without chickens. Targets food manufacturing applications where egg protein functionality (foaming, binding, emulsification) is required.

### New Culture

Produces **bovine beta-casein** through precision fermentation to create animal-free mozzarella with the same texture, flavor, and melting profile as conventional mozzarella. FDA regulatory approval received for their casein protein in the US.

## Relevance to the Mushroom Vault

Precision fermentation sits at the intersection of several vault topics:

- **[[Biotech Applications]]** — *T. reesei* and *A. niger* as industrial fermentation hosts
- **[[Mycoprotein]]** — traditional (biomass) vs. precision (target molecule) approaches to fungal protein
- **[[Koji Fermentation]]** — natural enzyme production that precision fermentation aims to optimize and redirect

The distinction matters for content creation: traditional fermentation produces the fungus itself as food (tempeh, mycoprotein); precision fermentation uses the fungus as a factory to produce something else (whey, casein, egg white).
