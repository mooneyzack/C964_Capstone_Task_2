# Tempeh and *Rhizopus oligosporus*

Tempeh is a traditional Indonesian fermented food produced by solid-state fermentation of cooked legumes (typically soybeans) with *Rhizopus oligosporus*. The mycelium physically penetrates and envelops the substrate particles, binding them into a compact, sliceable cake — making tempeh one of the simplest demonstrations of mycelium as a structural material. The fermentation simultaneously transforms the nutritional profile of the substrate, reducing antinutrients and increasing bioavailability of minerals and certain vitamins.

---

## Fermentation Process

*R. oligosporus* spores are inoculated onto cooked, dehulled soybeans and incubated at **30–35°C, 85–90% relative humidity** for **24–48 hours**. After 12–15 hours, endogenous heat production begins as the mycelium develops, requiring temperature management to prevent overheating.

**Binding mechanism**: The primary mechanism is mechanical — *R. oligosporus* produces a dense network of white hyphae that penetrate and envelop the substrate particles, physically knitting them together. Good tempeh shows beans bound by a continuous mat of white mycelium. Enzymatic modification of the substrate (proteolysis softening the beans) further enhances structural integration.

## Nutritional Transformation

Fermentation with *R. oligosporus* drives major changes:

| Transformation | Magnitude | Mechanism |
|---------------|-----------|-----------|
| Trypsin inhibitor reduction | 64–67% decrease | Enzymatic degradation |
| Phytate reduction | 29–33% | Phytase production by *R. oligosporus* |
| Mineral bioavailability | Increased (iron, zinc, calcium) | Phytic acid breakdown releases bound minerals |
| GABA production | ~21.4 mg/100g soybean per 24h | Glutamic acid decarboxylase activity |
| Protein transformation | Albumin, globulin degraded to amino acids and low-MW peptides | Protease secretion |

### Vitamin B12

*R. oligosporus* alone **does not produce B12**. However, co-fermentation with *Propionibacterium freudenreichii* produces cobalamin at **8.26 ± 0.13 µg/100g wet weight**. Synergistic effects between *Rhizopus* and *Propionibacterium* increased B12 up to **1,230 ng/g dry weight** in lupin tempeh — a finding with significant implications for plant-based nutrition.

## Novel Substrates

Research has evaluated over 25 substrates beyond soybean:

| Substrate | Notable Property |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Lupin (*Lupinus* spp.) | 32–53% protein dry matter; the B12 co-fermentation study substrate |
| Mung bean (*Vigna radiata*) | 137.53 mg GAE/g DM polyphenol content |
| Cowpea | 32.6% phytate reduction |
| Cereals, algae, seeds | Various; several showed superior nutritional characteristics vs. soybean tempeh |
