# Mycoprotein

Mycoprotein is protein derived from the biomass of filamentous fungi, produced via submerged or solid-state fermentation. Unlike plant-based meat alternatives that require extensive processing to mimic meat texture, filamentous fungi naturally produce fibrous hyphal networks that emulate the fibrous structure of animal muscle tissue — this is an intrinsic biological property, not an engineered feature.

The field spans established commercial products (Quorn, since 1985) to a wave of startups using different fungal species and production methods.

---

## Quorn — *Fusarium venenatum*

### Discovery and Production

*F. venenatum* was discovered in a field in Buckinghamshire, UK, during a 1960s screening program for organisms that could convert starch into protein. After 20 years of R&D and safety testing, commercial production began.

**Continuous fermentation process:**
- 150,000-liter pressure-cycle airlift fermenters, 40 meters high
- Produce approximately **300 kg biomass per hour**
- Fermenters run continuously for approximately **1,000 hours (~5 weeks)**
- Production runs are limited by the appearance of highly branched morphological mutants
- Post-fermentation: RNA reduction via heat treatment (to below 2%), steaming, chilling, freezing

### Nutritional Profile

| Component | Per 100g wet basis |
|-----------|--------------------|
| Protein | 11% (44% dry weight) |
| Fiber | 6% (⅔ beta-glucan, ⅓ chitin) |
| Fat | 2.9% |
| PDCAAS | Comparable to milk |
| Minerals | Iron, zinc, selenium, manganese, calcium, phosphorus |

All essential amino acids present; sulfur-containing amino acids (methionine, cysteine) are relatively low.

## Meati Foods — *Neurospora crassa*

*N. crassa* is a well-characterized model organism in genetics, also traditionally used in Indonesia to ferment **oncom** (a tempeh-like food). Meati's process: spores are placed in bioreactors with sugar/organic feedstocks and fermented. Dense mycelium biomass forms within days, then is pressed, shaped, and seasoned as whole-cut analogs (steaks, cutlets).

| Component | Per 100g |
|-----------|----------|
| Protein | 11–12.6g (PDCAAS 1.0 — matches animal protein quality) |
| Fiber | 5.3–6g |
| Fat | 1.0–2.9g |
| Notable nutrients | [[Ergothioneine]], zinc (7.6 mg), riboflavin, niacin, folate |

Available in 7,000+ retail locations. Meati and The Better Meat Co. are in an ongoing legal dispute over IP related to *N. crassa* cultivation for food.

## Nature's Fynd — *Fusarium strain flavolapis*

Founded from a 2009 NASA-funded study of extremophiles in Yellowstone National Park. Co-founder Mark Kozubal discovered this acidophilic fungus in geothermal hot springs. Originally called "MK7," later renamed *Fusarium strain flavolapis* (Latin: *flavo* = yellow, *lapis* = stone).

**Key differentiators:**
- Grows as a mycelial mat in **open trays** (not pressurized bioreactors) — simpler, potentially cheaper at scale
- "Fy Protein" contains all nine essential amino acids, vitamin B12, vitamin D, iron, calcium
- FDA GRAS "no questions" letter received 2021
- Uses **99% less land** and **87% less water** than beef production
- Profit-sharing agreement with Yellowstone National Park
- Backed by Bill Gates and Jeff Bezos; raised $500M+

## The Science of Texture

The meat-like texture of mycoprotein derives from the **filamentous nature of fungal hyphae**:

- Hyphal cells form fibrous bundles that emulate the fibrous structure of animal muscle tissue
- In *F. venenatum*, heat-treated hyphae become insoluble, with length and diameter suitable for products microscopically resembling chicken breast texture
- High fiber content (beta-glucan + chitin) contributes to structural integrity and mouthfeel
- This is a natural property of filamentous fungi — no texturizing agents or extrusion processing required (unlike pea/soy protein isolates)
