# Environmental Parameters

## Overview

Environmental parameters — temperature, humidity, CO2, fresh air exchange (FAE), and light — are the primary levers that control colonization speed, fruiting initiation, fruiting body development, and final yield. Parameters differ sharply between the colonization phase (mycelium growth) and the fruiting phase (pin formation and development). This note covers species-by-species parameter tables with exact values. See [[Fruiting Triggers]] for the biological mechanisms behind environmental cues.

## Details

### Pleurotus ostreatus — Pearl/Blue Oyster

| Parameter | Colonization | Fruiting |
|---|---|---|
| Temperature | 70–78°F (21–26°C) | 55–75°F (13–24°C) |
| Humidity | 70–80% | 85–95% |
| CO2 | 5,000–20,000 ppm | 500–1,000 ppm |
| FAE | 0–1 exchanges/hr | 4–8 exchanges/hr |
| Light | Not required | 500–1,000 lux, 12 hr/day |

**Substrate:** Straw (pasteurized), supplemented hardwood sawdust (sterilized), coffee grounds, cardboard.
**Timeline:** Grain colonization 7–14 days. Block colonization 10–21 days. Pins to harvest 5–10 days. First fruiting 3–5 weeks from inoculation. Subsequent flushes every 7–14 days.
**Special notes:** Oysters are the most FAE-demanding common species. CO2 must drop dramatically to initiate pinning. Primordia form in response to temperature drop, increased FAE, and humidity. Blue/pearl varieties prefer cooler fruiting temps (55–65°F). Pink and yellow oysters prefer warmer temps (65–75°F). High CO2 produces long, leggy stems with small caps; optimal FAE produces compact clusters with large, broad caps.
**Yield:** Biological efficiency 100–200% over 2–4 flushes. First flush typically 50–70% of total yield.

### Lentinula edodes — Shiitake

| Parameter | Colonization | Fruiting Induction | Fruiting |
|---|---|---|---|
| Temperature | 70–80°F (21–27°C) optimal 77°F | Cold shock 36–45°F (2–7°C) for 12–24 hrs | Cold strains: 50–65°F; Warm strains: 60–75°F |
| Humidity | 95–100% | — | 85–95% |
| CO2 | >10,000 ppm | — | <1,000 ppm |
| FAE | 0–1/hr | — | 4–8/hr |
| Light | 50–100 lux | — | 500–2,000 lux, 12 hr/day |

**Substrate:** Hardwood sawdust supplemented with wheat or rice bran (78:20:2 sawdust:bran:gypsum). Moisture: 48–50%. Sterilize 121°C for 2–4 hours. Logs (oak preferred).
**Timeline:** Spawn run on sawdust blocks: 60–90 days. Maturation/browning of block surface required before fruiting induction (additional 2–4 weeks). Cold soak (4–12°C water) for 12–24 hours triggers pinning. Pins to harvest: 7–14 days. Total first-flush cycle: 3–5 months on sawdust. On logs: 6–18 months to first flush, then every 4–8 weeks for years.
**Harvest timing:** Cap edge still in-rolled (60–70% cap opening), veil between gills and stem still intact on majority of mushrooms.
**Yield:** Biological efficiency 100–200% on supplemented sawdust over 4–6 flushes. On logs: 5–7+ flushes over 2–5 years.

### Hericium erinaceus — Lion's Mane

| Parameter | Colonization | Fruiting |
|---|---|---|
| Temperature | 70–75°F (21–24°C) | 65–70°F (18–21°C) |
| Humidity | 70–80% | 85–95% |
| CO2 | up to 5,000 ppm | 500–1,000 ppm |
| FAE | 0–1/hr | 5–8/hr |
| Light | Not required | 500–1,000 lux, 12 hr/day |

**Substrate:** Hardwood sawdust (oak, beech, maple) supplemented with 10–20% wheat bran or soybean hulls. Moisture: 60–65%. Sterilization required.
**Timeline:** Colonization: 14–28 days. Primordia initiation: 7–14 days after introducing fruiting conditions. Pin to harvest: 7–14 days. Total cycle: 6–8 weeks. 1–3 flushes per block.
**Harvest timing:** Harvest when spines (teeth) are 0.5–1 inch long and still white. Yellowing/browning indicates overmaturity — flavor becomes bitter. Do not wait for spore drop.
**Special notes:** [[Hericium erinaceus]] is extremely sensitive to CO2. High CO2 produces elongated, branched antler-like formations with poor quality. Adequate FAE is essential. Very sensitive to substrate contamination — sterilization failure is common cause of poor yields. Moisture content is critical; too wet = yellow, rot; too dry = no pinning.
**Yield:** Biological efficiency 80–150%. Typically 1–3 lbs fresh per 5 lb block over 1–2 flushes.

### Grifola frondosa — Maitake (Hen of the Woods)

| Parameter | Spawn Run | Dormancy | Fruiting |
|---|---|---|---|
| Temperature | 20–25°C (68–77°F) | Cool (10–15°C) | 50–65°F (10–18°C) |
| Humidity | 60–65% | — | 85–95% |
| CO2 | 20,000–40,000 ppm | — | <1,000 ppm |
| FAE | 0–1/hr | — | 4–6/hr |
| Light | 50 lux (optional) | — | 100–1,000 lux, 12 hr/day |

**Substrate:** Hardwood sawdust (oak strongly preferred) supplemented with wheat bran in 78:20:2 ratio (sawdust:bran:CaCO₃). Sterilize at 121°C for 60+ minutes. Moisture: 60–63%.
**Timeline:** Spawn run: 30–45 days. Mandatory cold dormancy period: 30 days at 10–15°C. Primordia initiation: 5–10 days. Stem formation: 10–14 days. Fruitbody development: 14–21 days. Total: 60–100+ days from inoculation.
**Special notes:** [[Grifola frondosa]] is the most difficult major cultivated species. Most wild isolates do not fruit reliably under cultivation — reliable production requires selected proprietary commercial strains. A temperature drop (cold shock) is mandatory for fruiting induction. Light functions as a molecular developmental switch, not merely orientation. Optimized corn cob substrate formulations achieve ~45% BE vs. 35% for standard oak sawdust.
**Yield:** Biological efficiency 35–45% under optimal conditions.

### Psilocybe cubensis

| Parameter | Colonization | Post-Casing | Primordia | Fruiting |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Temperature | 84–86°F (29–30°C) | 84–86°F | 74–78°F (23–26°C) | 74–78°F |
| Humidity | 90% | 90% | 95–100% | 85–92% |
| CO2 | 5,000–10,000 ppm | 5,000–10,000 ppm | <5,000 ppm | <5,000 ppm |
| FAE | 0/hr | 0/hr | 1–3/hr | 1–3/hr |
| Light | Total darkness | Total darkness | 12–16 hr/day blue-spectrum | 12–16 hr/day |

**Substrate:** Brown rice flour + vermiculite (PF Tek), or bulk CVG, manure, pasteurized straw/grain.
**Timeline:** PF Tek: colonization 14–21 days, pins in 6–10 days after fruiting conditions, harvest 3–7 days after pinning. Total: 5–7 weeks first flush. Flushes every 5–8 days. Production period 5 weeks: yields 2–4 lbs/sq ft with optimal management.
**Special notes:** Temperature drop from colonization (84°F) to fruiting (74–78°F) is a key pinning trigger. High CO2 during colonization is beneficial; CO2 must drop at fruiting. 12–16 hours of light (indirect or artificial) stimulates pinning.
**Yield:** Bulk: 15–30% BE per flush; 40–80% total across multiple flushes. Grows 3–5 flushes reliably before substrate exhaustion.

### Ganoderma lucidum — Reishi

| Parameter | Colonization | Antler Stage | Full Fruiting |
|---|---|---|---|
| Temperature | 72–82°F (22–28°C) | 77–86°F (25–30°C) | 70–80°F (21–27°C) |
| Humidity | 80–85% | 85–90% | 85–90% |
| CO2 | High (>5,000 ppm) | Low (<1,000 ppm) | <1,000 ppm |
| FAE | 0–1/hr | 2–4/hr | 2–4/hr |
| Light | Not required | 12 hr/day | 12 hr/day |

**Substrate:** Supplemented hardwood sawdust, oak preferred. Rice bran, wheat bran, sugarcane bagasse, coconut fiber can all be incorporated. Sterilization required. pH optimum: 4.5–5.5.
**Timeline:** Colonization: 10–14 days on sawdust blocks. Then 30–90+ days before conks fully develop. Total production timeline: 3 months to 1 year. Fruiting bodies develop extremely slowly by mushroom standards.
**Special notes:** [[Ganoderma lucidum]] develops in stages. First: finger-like "antler" projections — deep red to rust-brown with yellow/white growing tips. If CO2 is maintained high (antler stage), stalk elongates without cap formation. Reducing CO2 triggers cap development. For traditional flat conks: allow good FAE from start. For "antler" reishi (equal value): maintain CO2 >1,000 ppm during antler growth. Reishi is a polypore — the conk hardens and lacquers over time, eventually turning rust-red/brown and woody.
**Yield:** Biological efficiency 80–120% but yield in dry weight is low because reishi is ~90% water and the dried product is dense. Expect 1.5–2 lbs fresh per 5 lb block.

### Agaricus bisporus — Button / Crimini / Portobello

| Parameter | Phase I Compost | Phase II Compost | Spawn Run | Fruiting |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Temperature | 130–155°F (internal pile) | 140°F pasteurization then 120–128°F conditioning | 75–77°F | 60–66°F |
| Humidity | — | — | High | 85–95% |
| CO2 | — | — | — | 1,000–2,500 ppm |
| FAE | — | — | Moderate | Moderate |
| Light | Not required | — | Not required | Low (functional level) |

**Substrate:** Two-phase composted substrate. Phase I: wheat straw-bedded horse manure + poultry manure (nitrogen supplement) + gypsum. Compost pile 3–15 days with daily turning. Temperature rises to 155–175°F internally. Phase II: pasteurize at 140°F for 2+ hours, then condition at 120–128°F for 4–7 days (allows beneficial microbes to develop). Ammonia must be <0.05% at end of Phase II. Final nitrogen target: 2.0–2.5% dry weight.

**Casing layer:** Sphagnum peat moss + limestone, pH adjusted to 7.5. Critical — mushrooms cannot initiate without a casing layer. Apply 1.5–2 inches deep. Water regularly to maintain moisture without saturation.

**Timeline:** Composting: 2–3 weeks total (Phase I + II). Spawning: inoculate Phase II compost at 75°F. Colonization: 14–21 days. Casing application: after full colonization. Days to first pins after casing: 10–12 days. First harvest: 7–10 days after first pins. Harvest cycle: 2–4 day harvest window in a 7–10 day flush cycle. Total from composting start to first flush: 7–8 weeks.

**Yield:** 0.25–1.5+ lbs fresh per square foot per flush depending on management and supplementation.

### Flammulina velutipes — Enoki

| Parameter | Colonization | Fruiting (Cold) |
|---|---|---|
| Temperature | 64–77°F (18–25°C) | 45–61°F (7–16°C) |
| Humidity | 70–80% | 80–90% |
| CO2 | Moderate | >5,000 ppm for long stems |
| FAE | Minimal | Minimal (intentionally low) |
| Light | Not required | Near-darkness or very low light |

**Substrate:** Supplemented hardwood sawdust, wood chips, wheat straw.
**Timeline:** Colonization: 14–21 days. Temperature drop to 45–55°F triggers pinning in 5–10 days. Harvest: 7–10 days after entering fruiting conditions.
**Special notes:** The long, thin white stems of commercial enoki are achieved by intentionally maintaining high CO2 (>5,000 ppm) in darkness. The elevated CO2 suppresses cap development while promoting extreme stem elongation to 4–5 inches. Japanese cultivators use cylindrical paper or plastic inserts to guide stem growth. Wild/golden enoki is grown with more FAE and light, producing short, dark-capped, wide-stemmed mushrooms with stronger flavor. The cold temperature requirement is essential — [[Flammulina velutipes]] will not fruit at room temperature.
**Yield:** Biological efficiency 60–100% on supplemented sawdust.

### Pleurotus eryngii — King Oyster

| Parameter | Colonization | Fruiting |
|---|---|---|
| Temperature | 72–78°F (22–26°C) | 55–65°F (13–18°C) |
| Humidity | 70–80% | 85–95% |
| CO2 | 5,000–10,000 ppm | <2,000 ppm for cap development; intentionally higher for thick long stems |
| FAE | 0–1/hr | 2–4/hr (manipulated for stem control) |
| Light | Not required | 500–1,000 lux, 12 hr/day |

**Substrate:** Requires dense, supplemented substrate — straw alone insufficient. Hardwood sawdust + wheat bran + CaCO₃. Nitrogen content: 1.5–1.8%. Sterilization required.
**Timeline:** Colonization: 14–21 days. Fruiting conditions: pin formation in 7–14 days. First harvest: 7–14 days after pinning. Total from inoculation: 5–7 weeks. Second flush: 2–3 weeks after first.
**Special notes:** CO2 manipulation is used commercially to produce the characteristic thick, long stems: seal the grow bag until pins form (maintaining high CO2 = thick stems), then open to FAE for cap development. The result is the iconic king oyster shape — thick cylindrical stems with small caps. Yield approximately 1 lb per 5 lbs sterilized substrate (100–150% BE total across 2–3 flushes).

### Cordyceps militaris

| Parameter | Colonization | Primordia | Fruiting |
|---|---|---|---|
| Temperature | 60–70°F (15–21°C), in darkness | 16°C nights / 23°C days | 68–77°F (20–25°C) |
| Humidity | 80–85% | 90–95% | 90–95% |
| CO2 | — | — | Low |
| FAE | Minimal | Moderate | Moderate |
| Light | Total darkness | Begin 12–16 hr/day | 12–16 hr/day, ~500 lux |

**Substrate:** Brown rice (most common, highest cordycepin yield), autoclaved at 121°C for 30 minutes. Standard recipe: 20g rice + 32ml nutrient solution in 300ml bottle. Other substrates: oats, wheat, silkworm pupae (traditional), mixed grain.
**Timeline:** Colonization: 3–4 weeks in total darkness. Once rice is fully colonized (white), introduce 12–16 hours of light and lower temperature at night. Primordia form in 12–15 days after lighting begins. Fruiting body development: 4–6 weeks. Total: 8–12 weeks from inoculation.
**Special notes:** Unlike most cultivated mushrooms, [[Cordyceps militaris]] requires light as a developmental trigger — it will not form fruiting bodies without a photoperiod. Fruiting bodies are the orange "clubs" that project from the substrate. Harvest before orange tips begin to show white powdery spore production (when cordycepin content is highest). Maximum cordycepin production occurs at 25°C with pH 7.0.
**Yield:** Biological efficiency 20–45%. Primarily valued for medicinal compounds (cordycepin, adenosine) rather than biomass.
