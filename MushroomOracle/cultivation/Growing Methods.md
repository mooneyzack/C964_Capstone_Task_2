# Growing Methods

## Overview

Mushroom growing methods range from beginner-accessible jar cultures to commercial-scale automated facilities. Method choice depends on species, available equipment, space, and desired yield. Each method has distinct substrate requirements, environmental control needs, and yield profiles. See [[Substrates]] for substrate preparation and [[Environmental Parameters]] for species-specific conditions.

## Details

### PF Tek (Brown Rice Flour Cakes)

Developed in the early 1990s by "Psilocybe Fanaticus," PF Tek is the classic beginner method. Substrate recipe (ratio 2:1:1): 2 parts vermiculite + 1 part water + 1 part brown rice flour (BRF). Mix until clumps coat vermiculite. Fill half-pint mason jars to within 1/2 inch of rim without compressing. Top with 1/2 inch dry vermiculite as a contamination barrier. Sterilize 15 PSI for 60–75 minutes.

Inoculation: inject 1/4cc spore syringe through 4 holes in lid near the glass (for edge visibility), flaming needle between ports. Colonization: 2–4 weeks at 75–80°F in darkness. "Birthing": when fully colonized and primordia appear, invert jar, tap out cake intact. Soak in water 12–24 hours to rehydrate ("dunking"). Roll in dry vermiculite to maintain surface humidity. Place in fruiting chamber (SGFC) at 80%+ humidity, 70–75°F. Typical yield per cake: 4–8g dry per flush, 2–4 flushes. PF Tek is low-yield relative to bulk methods but requires minimal equipment.

### Monotub / Shoebox Tek

Bulk growing method using a modified plastic storage tote (commonly 66-quart Sterilite or similar). Core principle: colonized grain spawn is mixed with bulk casing substrate (CVG, manure, compost) in a tub, left to full colonization, then fruited without opening in a "set-and-forget" style. Holes drilled in sides (typically 4–6 holes, 1" diameter) and stuffed with polyfill for passive FAE. Alternatively, unmodified tubs with a gap under the lid ("SGFC" style). Substrate depth: 3–4 inches ideal. Spawn rate: 10–30% grain spawn to bulk substrate. Cover with a 1/4–1/2 inch casing layer (CVG). Colonization: 2–4 weeks at 75–80°F. Fruit by moving to 70–75°F, introducing more FAE. Yields: highly variable, 20–150g+ dry per flush over 2–4 flushes.

### Filter Patch Bag Culture

Polypropylene mushroom bags with 0.2-micron filter patches allow gas exchange while blocking contaminants. Used for both grain spawn production and bulk fruiting. Fill with sterilized substrate, leave 40% headspace, fold top and seal with impulse sealer or wire tie. After colonization, cut or open bag to expose to fruiting conditions. Best for wood-loving species ([[Lentinula edodes|shiitake]], [[Hericium erinaceus|lion's mane]], [[Pleurotus ostreatus|oyster]]). Bags can be fruited on shelves in a Martha tent. Commercial growers use 5–10 lb blocks in bags. Bags allow easy inspection — any contamination visible through the plastic.

### Log Inoculation (Plug Spawn / Totem Method)

**Plug Spawn Method:** Drill holes 1.5–2 inches deep using 5/16" or 12mm bit. Space holes in a diamond pattern every 6 inches along the log, rotating 2–3 inches around the circumference. Tap wooden plug spawn into each hole, then seal with cheese wax, beeswax, or food-grade wax using a dauber. Best done in early spring or late fall when the wood is at natural moisture content. Store in shaded, moist location. First fruiting: shiitake 6–12 months; oyster 3–6 months.

**Totem Method:** Stack 8–12 inch sections of fresh-cut log in a column, packing spawn sawdust between sections and at the top. The column is wrapped or placed in a bag to maintain humidity during colonization. Faster than plug spawn because the spawn interface is maximized.

**Log species selection:** [[Lentinula edodes]] (Shiitake) performs best on oak (Quercus spp.); oyster on cottonwood, alder, aspen, or almost any hardwood; lion's mane on beech or maple; reishi on oak, hemlock.

### Outdoor Bed Cultivation

Best for wine caps (Stropharia rugosoannulata), oysters, and King Stropharia. Prepare beds in shaded areas using wood chips (wood-loving species) or straw. Layer 4–6 inches of pasteurized or simply dampened substrate. Spread grain or sawdust spawn at 10–15% rate. Cover with more substrate (1–2 inches). Maintain moisture by watering in dry periods. First fruiting: 2–6 months. Beds can produce for 2–5 years with occasional top-dressing of fresh wood chips.

### Martha Tent / Fruiting Chamber

A vertical wire-shelf greenhouse (typically 4–5 tiers, 40"x20"x60") enclosed in a clear plastic cover. Components:
- Ultrasonic or cool-mist humidifier (positioned outside tent, mist ducted inside)
- Inkbird IHC-200 or similar humidity controller (set trigger at 85%, shutoff at 93%)
- Two PC/computer fans: one near the bottom (intake) and one near the top (exhaust)
- Hygrometer/thermometer placed mid-shelf for accurate readings
- HEPA filter on intake fan polyfill cover

Environmental targets: humidity 85–95% RH; fresh air exchange 3–6 complete cycles per hour; temperature 65–75°F for most species. Species on shelves: colonized blocks, bags, or trays are arranged on wire shelves, misted until developing pins reach harvest size. Ideal for oyster, shiitake, lion's mane, and reishi. Highly scalable — a single 4-shelf Martha can fruit 4–12 blocks simultaneously. Cost: $75–$200 for basic setup; automated versions $150–$400.

---

### Bucket Tek (Oyster Mushrooms)

Fill 5-gallon buckets with pasteurized straw or supplemented hardwood, inoculate with grain spawn or plug spawn. Drill 4–6 holes (1/2 to 2-inch diameter) on the sides of the bucket at varying heights. Fruiting bodies emerge through the holes. Stack buckets vertically in a humid environment. Simple, low-cost, high-yield method ideal for [[Pleurotus ostreatus|oyster mushrooms]] (Pleurotus spp.). Biological efficiency 80–150%. Easy to manage multiple flushes by resting and rehydrating between harvests.

---

### Commercial-Scale Growing

**Shelf Systems:** Typically double-sided metal shelving with adjustable levels. Blocks or trays sit on shelves spaced 12–18 inches apart. Some facilities use rolling rack carts for mobility between colonization rooms and fruiting rooms.

**Automated Environmental Controls:** Commercial grow rooms use:
- HVAC with humidity injection (steam injection or evaporative systems) to maintain 85–95% RH
- CO2 monitors (infrared sensor, 0–5000 ppm range) wired to intake/exhaust fan controllers
- Programmable logic controllers (PLCs) or commercial controllers (Trolmaster, Inkbird Pro) for automated cycling
- Chilled water systems for temperature regulation
- Flood irrigation or overhead misting for humidity
- LED or fluorescent lighting on timers (12 hours on/off)

**Typical commercial yields:** 0.25–1.5 lbs fresh mushrooms per square foot per flush ([[Agaricus bisporus]]); 1–3 lbs per 5 lb block per flush (oyster, lion's mane).
