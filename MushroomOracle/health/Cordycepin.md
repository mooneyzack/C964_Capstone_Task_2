## Overview

Cordycepin (3′-deoxyadenosine) is the signature bioactive nucleoside of *[[Cordyceps militaris]]* and the defining compound that distinguishes cultivated *C. militaris* from wild *Ophiocordyceps sinensis*. It is structurally similar to adenosine but **lacks the 3′-hydroxyl group**, giving it distinct pharmacological properties.

**Important:** Wild *Ophiocordyceps sinensis* (caterpillar fungus) does NOT contain significant cordycepin — contrary to many commercial claims. This was confirmed definitively in a 2017 biosynthetic study. Most modern supplement research and production uses *C. militaris*.

## Chemical Structure

- **IUPAC name:** 3′-deoxyadenosine
- **Structural analogy:** Adenosine analog; the missing 3′-OH prevents nucleotide chain extension in RNA
- **Biosynthesis:** Produced via the Cns1–Cns4 gene cluster in *C. militaris*; first isolated in 1950

The missing hydroxyl group is the key pharmacological feature: as a pseudosubstrate for adenosine in RNA synthesis, cordycepin acts as a **chain terminator** when incorporated into RNA.

## Pharmacological Activities

### Anti-Tumor
- Acts as a chain terminator of RNA synthesis (pseudo-substrate for adenosine in RNA)
- Induces apoptosis in multiple cancer cell lines
- Mechanism: inhibits mRNA polyadenylation, blocking protein synthesis in target cells

### Anti-Inflammatory
- Inhibits LPS-induced NF-κB activation
- Suppresses TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-6 production
- Shown to suppress T-cell activation with potential relevance to COPD therapy

### Antiviral
- Inhibits viral RNA replication (same chain-termination mechanism as antitumor activity)

### ATP Enhancement and Cardiovascular
- Structurally related to adenosine precursors; proposed to enhance cellular ATP production
- Adenosine-like effects on the cardiovascular system via enhanced adenosine-mediated vasodilation
- May inhibit platelet aggregation
- Enhanced blood flow and nitric oxide pathways — proposed mechanism behind cardiovascular and athletic performance benefits

### Respiratory Function
- Cordycepin suppresses T-cell activation, potentially reducing chronic airway inflammation in COPD
- Animal studies show bronchodilatory and anti-inflammatory effects in airway tissue

### Energy Metabolism
- As a 3′-deoxyadenosine, affects cellular nucleotide pools and may influence mitochondrial biogenesis
- Increases in ATP production observed in animal and cell studies
- Polysaccharides from *C. militaris* demonstrate anti-fatigue effects in forced swim tests in animal models

## Clinical Evidence (Cordyceps-Containing Products)

While most cordycepin-specific research is preclinical, *C. militaris*-containing products have been evaluated in human trials:

**Athletic performance — Key RCT (Hirsch et al., 2017, PMC5236007):**
- Design: 28 participants, double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled
- Intervention: 4g/day mushroom blend (*C. militaris*) vs. maltodextrin
- 3-week results: VO₂max improved +4.8 ml·kg⁻¹·min⁻¹ in mushroom group (p=0.042) vs. +0.9 ml·kg⁻¹·min⁻¹ in placebo
- 1-week results: No significant difference
- Conclusion: Consistent 4g/day supplementation over 3 weeks significantly improved maximal oxygen consumption and exercise tolerance

**Older adults — Cs-4 extract RCT (Chen et al.):**
- Double-blind, placebo-controlled trial in healthy older subjects
- Cs-4 (fermented *C. sinensis* extract) improved aerobic capacity and VO₂max vs. placebo over 12 weeks

**Respiratory:**
- Cordyceps supplementation reduced symptoms in a randomized study of COPD patients, improving forced expiratory volume

## Dosing Reference

| Form | Dose in Clinical Studies | Notes |
|---|---|---|

## Extraction Notes

Cordycepin is reasonably water-soluble; hot water extraction captures most cordycepin from *C. militaris* fruiting bodies. Alcohol extraction can capture additional triterpenoid compounds. See [[Extraction Methods]] for details.
