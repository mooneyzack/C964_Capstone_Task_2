## Overview

Hericenones and erinacines are the defining bioactive compounds of lion's mane (*[[Hericium erinaceus]]*), responsible for its neurotrophic activity. They are the only known dietary/supplement compounds that stimulate **Nerve Growth Factor (NGF)** synthesis in the brain. Critically, these two compound families come from **different parts of the mushroom** — a distinction with major implications for product quality evaluation.

## Hericenones (Fruiting Body)

Hericenones are aromatic compounds found in the **fruiting body** of lion's mane: aromatic hericenone A through H, plus hericenones C–H including newer additions.

**Mechanism:** Hericenones do not directly stimulate NGF gene expression, but they:
- Promote NGF-stimulated neurite outgrowth by sensitizing neurons to available NGF
- Hericenone C has demonstrated NGF synthesis promotion in certain cell lines

**Note:** Because hericenones are found in the fruiting body, hot water or ethanol extraction of fruiting body material will contain them. However, their activity is potentiating (sensitizing neurons) rather than directly inducing NGF synthesis — making erinacines the more pharmacologically significant class for neuroprotection.

## Erinacines (Mycelium Only)

Erinacines are **cyathin diterpenoids** found **primarily in the mycelia** (not fruiting body). 15 variants have been identified (A–K, P–S).

### Key Erinacines and Their Activities

| Erinacine | Primary Activity |
|---|---|
| **Erinacine A** | Most potent — induces ~250 pg/mL NGF synthesis at 1 mM in vitro; reduced amyloid-β plaques and increased IDE expression by 130–303% in AD models |
| **Erinacine B** | Stimulates NGF gene expression |
| **Erinacine C** | Stimulates NGF gene expression |
| **Erinacine E** | Stimulates NGF gene expression; manages neuropathic pain via opioid receptor pathways |
| **Erinacine S** | Stimulates neurite outgrowth; increases IDE (insulin-degrading enzyme) expression |

### NGF Stimulation Mechanism

Erinacines cross the **blood-brain barrier** and stimulate NGF synthesis in the hippocampus and cerebellum. NGF (Nerve Growth Factor) promotes:
- Survival, maintenance, and regeneration of neurons
- Differentiation and maturation of cholinergic neurons (relevant to Alzheimer's pathology)
- Axon regrowth after peripheral nerve injury

## In Vivo Efficacy Data (Preclinical)

All neuroprotection and NGF-stimulation research with erinacines is preclinical (cell culture and animal models) as of 2024:

- **Stroke model:** 44% reduction in cerebral infarct volume at higher doses
- **Parkinson's model:** Reversal of MPTP-induced motor deficits
- **Alzheimer's model:** Reduced amyloid plaque burden; increased IDE levels by 130–303%
- **Depression models:** Normalized BDNF pathways
- **Peripheral nerve injury models:** Accelerated nerve regeneration and functional recovery

**Dose context:** Human mycelium-enriched products typically dosed at 50–400 mg/kg in animal studies; clinical trial oral doses range from 250–500 mg/day mycelium extract to 1–3g/day standardized fruiting body powder.

## Human Clinical Evidence

See [[Hericium erinaceus]] species page for full clinical trial summaries. Key points:

- **2009 RCT (Mori et al.):** 3g/day dried fruiting body powder for 16 weeks; significant improvement on Hasegawa's Dementia Scale in mild cognitive impairment (N=30, ages 50–80); effects reversed after 4-week washout
- **2023 RCT:** 1.8g/day for 28 days; trend toward reduced stress; cognitive improvements in specific tasks observed acutely
- Depression/anxiety: 4-week RCT in menopausal women showed significant reduction in depression vs. placebo

**Assessment:** The most credible human evidence is for mild cognitive impairment in the elderly. Erinacine-specific human trials are limited. Larger phase 2/3 trials are needed.

## Critical Distinction: Product Sourcing Matters

This is the most important practical implication of the hericenone/erinacine distinction:

| Compound | Source | Extraction |
|---|---|---|
| Hericenones | Fruiting body | Hot water or ethanol |
| Erinacines | Mycelium only | Requires mycelium source; diterpenoids may need alcohol |

**A product claiming "lion's mane benefits" for neuroprotection should:**
1. Specify whether it uses fruiting body, mycelium, or both
2. Ideally test for both compound classes
3. If claiming erinacine content, the source must be mycelium — fruiting body does not contain erinacines

Many commercial lion's mane products use fruiting body only and thus contain no erinacines. Conversely, mycelium-on-grain (MoG) products may contain erinacines but have low beta-glucan content due to grain starch dilution. See [[Extraction Methods]] for the fruiting body vs. mycelium debate.
