# Microdosing Evidence

Microdosing — consuming sub-perceptual doses of psychedelics (typically 1/10th to 1/20th of a full dose) on a repeating schedule — has generated enormous popular interest. The controlled scientific evidence, however, consistently shows that reported benefits are **largely or entirely explained by expectancy and placebo effects**.

This page covers what the controlled evidence actually shows. For full-dose clinical research, see [[Psilocybin Research]].

---

## The Self-Blinding Citizen Science Trial (Szigeti et al., 2021)

The single most important controlled study on microdosing. Published in *eLife*, 191 completers — the largest placebo-controlled psychedelic study at the time. Participants were existing microdosers who created their own blinded capsules (some microdose, some placebo) using online instructions.

**Results:**
- All psychological outcomes (well-being, mindfulness, life satisfaction, paranoia) **improved significantly from baseline in all groups — including placebo**
- **No significant between-group differences** on any accumulative outcome
- Small acute differences (mood +4.6, energy +5.3, creativity +5.2 points) were statistically significant but clinically marginal (Cohen's d < 0.3)
- Participants broke blind at a **72% rate** (vs. 62% expected by chance)
- When "guess" was modeled as a covariate, **virtually all psychological differences disappeared** except subjective drug intensity
- **Cognitive performance** (the only non-self-rated outcome) showed **zero differences** in any condition
- A subgroup of participants with lowest baseline well-being showed identical null results

**Authors' conclusion**: "The findings suggest that anecdotal benefits of microdosing can be explained by the placebo effect."

---

## Controlled Laboratory Studies

A 2024 review in *Biological Psychiatry: Cognitive Neuroscience and Neuroimaging* compiled 14 controlled LSD microdosing studies (5–20 µg doses):

- Acute microdoses dose-dependently altered blood pressure, sleep, neural connectivity, social cognition, mood, and perception of pain and time
- Perceptible drug effects at 10–20 µg but not 5 µg
- **Repeated doses of LSD did not alter mood or cognition** on any studied measure
- No serious adverse effects in any controlled study
- Nearly all participants were young, educated, Western, male, and White — limiting generalizability

---

## 2025 Double-Blind Longitudinal Trials

Two double-blind placebo-controlled longitudinal psilocybin microdosing trials (2025) assessed subjective experience, social cognition, and mental health outcomes. Results continued the pattern of **mixed findings with expectancy effects**.

---

## The Bottom Line

| Claim | Evidence Status |
|-------|----------------|
| Microdosing improves well-being | Controlled studies show improvements in all groups including placebo; no between-group differences |
| Microdosing enhances cognition | Objective cognitive measures show **no enhancement** |
| Microdosing treats depression | No controlled psilocybin-specific microdosing study with lab-supplied material has shown clear benefits beyond placebo |
| Microdosing is safe | Controlled studies report no serious adverse effects |

The field honestly acknowledges that microdosing research remains "fragmented" with "scattered significant findings across disparate domains."

---

## Context for Content Creators

This is a topic where public belief significantly outpaces the evidence. Responsible content should:
- Acknowledge the popularity and widespread anecdotal reports
- Present the self-blinding trial as the key counterpoint
- Distinguish between microdosing (sub-perceptual, evidence weak) and full-dose therapy (clinical-dose, strong Phase 3 evidence — see [[Psilocybin Research]])
- Avoid conflating the two
