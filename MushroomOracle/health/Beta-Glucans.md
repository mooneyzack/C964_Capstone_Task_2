## Overview

Beta-glucans are polysaccharides composed of D-glucose monomers linked by β-type glycosidic bonds. They are the **primary immunomodulatory compound class** in medicinal mushrooms, acting through dedicated pattern-recognition receptors on innate immune cells. Fungal beta-glucans are structurally and functionally distinct from the beta-glucans found in oats and barley.

## Structural Distinction: Fungal vs. Cereal Beta-Glucans

- **Cereal beta-glucans** (oat, barley): β-1,3 and β-1,4 linkages — linear, no branching
- **Fungal/mushroom beta-glucans**: β-1,3 backbone with β-1,6 branching side chains — the branching creates a **triple-helix tertiary conformation** that is critical for biological activity

The triple-helix conformation is recognized by innate immune pattern recognition receptors as a pathogen-associated molecular pattern (PAMP), triggering a coordinated immune response.

## Receptor Mechanisms

**1. Dectin-1** (C-type lectin-like receptor)
Primary receptor, expressed on monocytes, macrophages, neutrophils, and dendritic cells. Binding activates spleen tyrosine kinase (Syk) and the CARD9-Bcl10-MALT1 (CBM) complex, triggering NF-κB activation, cytokine production (TNF-α, IL-6, IL-12, IL-23), and enhanced phagocytosis.

**2. Complement Receptor 3 (CR3 / CD11b/CD18)**
After macrophage uptake and partial digestion of beta-glucan particles into smaller fragments, these fragments enter the lymphatic circulation and bind CR3 on neutrophils and NK cells, priming them for enhanced cytotoxicity against antibody-opsonized tumor cells — the "CR3-dependent cytotoxicity" mechanism relevant to cancer research.

**3. Toll-like receptors (TLR-2, TLR-6)**
Co-activation of TLRs with Dectin-1 potentiates cytokine output.

## Named Beta-Glucan Fractions

Different species contain distinct, named fractions with characterized properties:

- **Lentinan**: a highly purified β-1,3-D-glucan with β-1,6 branches from shiitake — approved as an injectable drug in Japan for cancer treatment since 1985
- **PSK (Polysaccharide-K / Krestin)** and **PSP (Polysaccharide Peptide)**: protein-bound polysaccharides from turkey tail; PSK approved as a prescription cancer adjunct in Japan since 1977
- **D-fraction and MD-fraction**: specific beta-glucan fractions from maitake, isolated by Dr. Hiroaki Nanba — distinct from crude extracts in potency and receptor specificity
- **Grifolan (GRN)**: a gel-forming (1→6)-branched (1→3)-β-D-glucan from maitake
- **Pleuran**: beta-glucan fraction from oyster mushroom, studied for respiratory health
- **Ganoderans A, B, C**: heteropolymers from reishi containing glucose, xylose, mannose, galactose, fucose with β-1,3/1,6 linkages

## Clinical Evidence

**Cancer adjunct therapy:**
- Intravenous lentinan (shiitake beta-glucan) at 1–1.5 mg/day for 2–8 weeks, combined with chemotherapy, significantly prolonged survival in advanced gastric cancer meta-analysis (N=650 patients)
- Lung cancer trials: 56% vs. 43.3% response rates with lentinan adjunct therapy
- PSK (turkey tail) demonstrates consistent survival benefits in multiple Japanese RCTs for Stage I–III colorectal and gastric cancer at 3g/day oral dose

**Immunomodulation:**
- Oral mushroom extracts at ~3.5g/day of fungal beta-glucans demonstrate immunomodulatory effects in human subjects
- Reishi Cochrane review (2016, N=373): CD3 T-cells +3.91%, CD4 T-cells +3.05%, CD8 T-cells +2.02% in cancer patients

**Gut microbiome:**
- PSP from turkey tail produced "clear and consistent microbiome changes consistent with prebiotic activity" — increased Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium while significantly inhibiting Clostridiales (Pallav et al., 2014, Gut Microbes)

**Respiratory health:**
- Pleuran (oyster mushroom beta-glucan) in pediatric trials reduced peripheral blood eosinophilia and stabilized IgE levels in respiratory illness

## Extraction Notes

Beta-glucans are water-soluble and require **hot water extraction** to liberate them from the chitin cell wall matrix. Without this step, the majority of beta-glucans remain locked in the cell wall and are inaccessible. Alcohol extraction can cause beta-glucan precipitation, reducing content. See [[Extraction Methods]] for full detail.

**Quality note:** When evaluating supplements, look for products that specify beta-glucan content (not just "total polysaccharides") measured by validated assay (HPGPC). Mycelium-on-grain products may report high "total polysaccharides" where most of that content is grain starch (alpha-glucans), not immunologically active beta-glucans.
