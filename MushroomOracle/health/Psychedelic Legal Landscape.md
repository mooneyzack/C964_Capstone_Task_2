# Psychedelic Legal Landscape

The legal status of psilocybin is shifting rapidly across multiple jurisdictions. This page tracks the regulatory frameworks that are furthest along. For the clinical research underlying these policy changes, see [[Psilocybin Research]].

---

## United States

### Oregon — Measure 109 (First U.S. Regulated Access)

Approved by voters November 2020. Service centers began opening summer 2023.

**By Q1 2025:**
- ~16,000 total clients served; 37,000+ psilocybin products sold
- ~24 service centers, 377 licensed facilitators
- Revenue exceeding $1.7 million
- Only **6 adverse reactions** reported out of 1,509 Q1 2025 clients
- **Market contraction**: 12 of 35 licensed centers have closed since early 2024 due to high operational costs ($10,000 annual license fees, mandated security infrastructure)
- Most clients seek services for depression, anxiety, PTSD, or personal growth
- Facilitator training requirement: 120 hours (critics argue this may be insufficient)
- No post-session monitoring required

### Colorado — Natural Medicine Health Act (Proposition 122)

Approved November 2022. Implementation bill SB23-290 passed May 2023.

- License applications accepted from **December 31, 2024**
- Rules finalized for facilitator licensing, healing centers, cultivators, and testing as of August 2025
- **Two license types**: full healing centers (dedicated facilities) and micro-healing centers (add-on for existing mental health practices, including home visits)
- Until June 1, 2026, "natural medicine" means only psilocybin and psilocin; DMT, ibogaine, and mescaline (excluding peyote) may be added after that date if the Advisory Board recommends
- **Personal use**: Adults 21+ may cultivate psilocybin mushrooms in a 12' × 12' locked space with no possession limit

### FDA Pathway

- Psilocybin holds **Breakthrough Therapy Designation** for both TRD (COMPASS, 2018) and MDD (Usona, 2019)
- COMPASS targeting rolling NDA submission **Q4 2026**, potential FDA decision late 2026 or early 2027
- FDA granted **priority review vouchers** to psychedelic companies in April 2026
- If approved, DEA would reschedule the specific formulation (not psilocybin generally) within 90 days, following the Epidiolex (CBD) precedent from 2018

## International

### Australia — TGA Rescheduling

Australia became the **first country** to formally reschedule psilocybin and MDMA as medicines (effective July 1, 2023). Psilocybin moved to Schedule 8 for treatment-resistant depression; MDMA for PTSD.

**Requirements**: Prescribing requires authorized psychiatrists with TGA Authorised Prescriber status and human research ethics committee approval.

**Uptake**: Slow — at least 10 authorized psychiatrists by early 2024. Barriers include lack of trained staff, no standardized training, specialized treatment spaces needed, and high costs.

In March 2025, Mind Medicine Australia submitted a rescheduling application to expand psilocybin access to end-of-life existential distress.

### Canada — Special Access Programme (SAP)

Health Canada amended regulations January 2022 to include psilocybin and MDMA in the SAP. Authorized for end-of-life distress and treatment-resistant MDD (psilocybin) and PTSD (MDMA).

**Approvals**: 301 total since inception (56 in 2022, 106 in 2023, 104 in 2024, 35 in first half of 2025). Approval rates have **dropped sharply** under the Carney government.

A Federal Court ruled in 2025 that Health Canada's refusal to allow experiential psilocybin training for healthcare workers was "unreasonable."
