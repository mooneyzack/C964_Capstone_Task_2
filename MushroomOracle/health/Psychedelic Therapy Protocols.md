# Psychedelic Therapy Protocols

This page describes the therapeutic model used in clinical psilocybin trials. Virtually all research programs use the same three-phase structure, and the therapeutic setting is considered inseparable from the pharmacological intervention. For the pharmacology and clinical trial results, see [[Psilocybin Research]].

---

## Three-Phase Structure

### 1. Preparation (1–3 sessions, 1–2 hours each)

- Explore the patient's history, symptoms, and treatment goals
- Education about psilocybin's effects and what to expect
- Build therapeutic alliance — rapport is considered essential
- Discuss "set and setting" (the patient's mindset and physical environment)
- Establish intentions without rigid expectations
- Introduce the concept of "letting go" and trusting the experience

### 2. Dosing Session (1–2 sessions, 6–8 hours each)

- Conducted in a comfortable, living-room-like setting (not a clinical ward)
- Patient lies on a couch or bed wearing **eyeshades and headphones**
- **Curated music playlist** plays throughout (see below)
- Two trained practitioners remain present for the entire duration
- Approach is **non-directive and supportive** — therapists do not guide or interpret the experience in real time
- Meditation and breathwork tools available if anxiety arises
- Session lasts until drug effects substantially dissipate (6–8 hours)
- Standard dose: **25 mg synthetic psilocybin** (COMPASS) or equivalent

### 3. Integration (2–4 sessions over subsequent weeks)

- Patient discusses and processes the experience with the therapist
- Emphasis on making meaning from insights, imagery, and emotions
- Connection of experience to ongoing life patterns and therapeutic goals
- Behavioral changes encouraged and supported

## The Music Playlist

Music is considered a "crucial tool" in the protocol. Bill Richards (Johns Hopkins) describes it as "a nonverbal support system, sort of like the net for a trapeze artist."

**Johns Hopkins playlist**: 7 hours 40 minutes, structured in segments — background during arrival, ascent music as effects begin, peak music (the most emotionally intense classical and orchestral works), post-peak, and "welcome back" music. Composers include Mozart, Bach, Vivaldi, Górecki.

Usona Institute adapted the playlist (7 pieces from the Hopkins list). NYU Langone developed its own variant.

## FDA Requirements

The FDA is expected to require a **REMS** (Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategy) for any approved psilocybin medicine:

- **Supervised administration only** (not take-home)
- Licensed medical professionals present during dosing
- Clinical trials use a **two-practitioner model** (fully trained psychotherapist, often M.D., plus a second facilitator)
- Specific qualifications for psychedelic therapists remain under development
- REMS addresses safety but not psychotherapy quality

A validated, universally agreed-upon protocol does not yet exist — less than half of reviewed trials reported standardized procedures, manualized treatments, or adherence monitoring. The **Yale Manual for Psilocybin-Assisted Therapy** is one attempt at standardization.

---

## Key Limitation: The Setting Confound

The therapeutic setting (preparation, music, therapist rapport, integration) makes it difficult to isolate the pharmacological contribution of psilocybin. Psilocybin's therapeutic effects (lasting weeks to months) far outlast its pharmacokinetic effects (under 12 hours), strongly implicating **neuroplasticity** as the bridge from acute experience to lasting change (see [[Psilocybin Research]]).
